lasagna

Description

Everyone loves lasagna and our simple recipe is so easy to make - the pasta and sauce come together perfectly without much effort on your part. Just make sure to keep the heat low so the pasta doesn't overcook and the bottom doesn't burn. You can make the sauce ahead of time - just reheat and continue with the recipe.

Preparation time

  • Preparation time: 1 hour
  • Total time: 1 hour 30 minutes
  • Servings: 6

Kitchen utensils:

  • Measuring spoons
  • Sharp knife (adult must use)
  • Cutting board
  • Measuring cup
  • Can opener
  • Square grater (adult must use)
  • Large skillet with lid (10 inches/25.4 cm)
  • Heat-resistant spatula

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive or vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, peeled, finely chopped or minced
  • 2 medium zucchini (Italian), ends trimmed, diced
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil or oregano
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (28 ounces), including liquid
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • 2 cups fresh zucchini leaves spinach, roughly chopped
  • 8 lasagna sheets (that do not need to be boiled first), halved
  • ¼ cup ricotta cheese
  • ¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • ¼ cup grated mozzarella or Monterey Jack cheese
  • ½ cup chopped fresh basil leaves

Preparation

  1. To prepare the sauce: Place the skillet on the stove and turn the heat to low to medium. When the skillet is hot, add the oil. Add the onion and garlic and cook, stirring frequently until soft, 10 to 15 minutes.
  2. Add the zucchini, basil or oregano and cook, covered, until the zucchini is golden and soft, 10 to 15 minutes, stirring every 5 minutes.
  3. Add the tomatoes and water, stir well. Reduce the heat to low and cook with the lid on for 10 minutes. Remove the lid and cook for about 10 minutes more.
  4. Turn off the heat, add the spinach and stir until the spinach is cooked.
  5. Carefully slide 4 pieces of lasagna into the pan, using the edges to slide them to the bottom of the sauce with the spatula, pushing them down to the bottom. Add another layer of 4 pieces of lasagna, then another 4 and pushing them down to the surface. Add the last 4 pieces of lasagna and spoon some of the tomato mixture over the top.
  6. Add the ricotta cheese, 1 tablespoon at a time, dropping it onto the top. Sprinkle with the mozzarella cheese and then the Parmesan cheese.
  7. Carefully slide 4 pieces of lasagna into the pan, using the edges to slide them to the bottom of the sauce with the spatula, pushing them down to the bottom. Add another layer of 4 pieces of lasagna, then another 4 and pushing them down to the surface. Add the last 4 pieces of lasagna and spoon some of the tomato mixture over the top.
  8. Reheat the pan over low heat, cover and continue cooking until the cheese has melted and the pasta is tender when pierced with the tip of a knife, about 20 minutes. Sprinkle with the basil leaves. Turn off heat and let sit for 10 minutes.
  9. Serve immediately or cover and refrigerate for up to 2 days.
